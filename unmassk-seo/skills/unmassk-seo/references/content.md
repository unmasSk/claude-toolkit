# Content Quality Reference

On-demand reference for content quality audits. Covers E-E-A-T evaluation, content minimums by page type, AI content assessment, Helpful Content System integration, AI citation readiness, and keyword optimization.

---

## E-E-A-T Framework

Updated per Google Quality Rater Guidelines — September 11, 2025, with December 2025 Core Update implications.

E-E-A-T = **Experience**, **Expertise**, **Authoritativeness**, **Trustworthiness**

Trustworthiness is the most important factor. It is assessed based on the other three signals plus direct trust indicators.

### Scoring Weights

| Factor | Weight | Primary Signals |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Experience | 20% | First-hand signals, original content, case studies |
| Expertise | 25% | Author credentials, technical accuracy, depth |
| Authoritativeness | 25% | External recognition, citations, reputation |
| Trustworthiness | 30% | Contact info, transparency, security, policies |

### December 2025 Core Update — Watershed Moment

E-E-A-T now applies to ALL competitive queries, not just YMYL. The December 2025 core update:
- Extended E-E-A-T evaluation to virtually all competitive queries
- Made author attribution standards tighter across all categories
- Penalized anonymous or generic authorship even for non-YMYL content
- Significantly improved AI content quality detection

**Measured traffic impact by industry:**

| Industry | Average Traffic Decline |
|----------|----------------------|
| Affiliate sites | 71% |
| Health/YMYL | 67% |
| E-commerce | 52% |

**Key takeaway:** Even entertainment and lifestyle content now requires demonstrated expertise. Generic content no longer ranks.

## Experience (Weight: 20%)

First-hand knowledge and personal involvement with the topic. This dimension is the key differentiator elevated by the December 2025 update — AI can generate expertise-sounding content but cannot fabricate genuine experience.

### Signals to Evaluate

- Author has demonstrable first-hand experience with the topic
- Content includes original photos, screenshots, or proprietary data
- Case studies or real-world examples with specific, verifiable details
- Personal process documentation or methodology descriptions
- Before/after results or measurable outcome data
- Specific anecdotes that could not be fabricated
- First-person narrative ("I tested this...", "In my experience...")
- Original photos and screenshots (not stock images)

### Scoring Guide

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Strong | Multiple first-hand experience signals, original content, verifiable details |
| Moderate | Some personal experience evident, partial documentation |
| Weak | Generic information, no personal touch, stock imagery |
| None | Clearly AI-generated or scraped content with no experiential markers |

---

## Expertise (Weight: 25%)

Formal qualifications, training, and demonstrated knowledge depth.

### Signals to Evaluate

- Author credentials relevant to the topic (bio, certifications, degrees)
- Technical accuracy and depth appropriate for the target audience
- Claims supported by evidence, citations, or sources
- Specialized vocabulary used correctly and naturally
- Up-to-date with current developments in the field
- Byline with author name and credentials visible on the page
- Professional background demonstrably relevant to the topic

### Scoring Guide

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Strong | Verified credentials, deep technical accuracy, cited sources |
| Moderate | Demonstrable knowledge, some credentials, mostly accurate |
| Weak | Surface-level information, no credentials, limited depth |
| None | Factual errors, misinformation, contradicts established knowledge |

---

## Authoritativeness (Weight: 25%)

Recognition by others as a go-to source in the topic area.

### Signals to Evaluate

- Site recognized as an authority in its niche
- Author recognized as expert (external citations, speaking engagements, publications)
- Content cited by other authoritative sources
- Industry awards, certifications, or accreditations
- Consistent publication history in the topic area
- Featured in reputable media outlets
- Professional affiliations and memberships
- Backlink profile from relevant, authoritative domains

### Scoring Guide

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Strong | Widely recognized authority, cited by others, industry presence |
| Moderate | Growing recognition, some external validation, niche authority |
| Weak | No external recognition, isolated content, no citations |
| None | Negative reputation, known for misinformation, penalized domain |

---

## Trustworthiness (Weight: 30%)

Overall reliability and transparency — the most important E-E-A-T factor.

### Signals to Evaluate

- Clear contact information (physical address, phone number, email)
- Privacy policy and terms of service accessible
- HTTPS with valid certificate (no mixed content)
- Transparent about who creates content and why
- Customer reviews and testimonials
- Corrections and update history visible
- No deceptive practices (hidden ads, clickbait, misleading headlines)
- Secure payment processing (for e-commerce)
- Return/refund policy visible (for e-commerce)
- Date stamps on content (publication and last updated)

### Scoring Guide

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Strong | Full transparency, verified business, positive reputation, complete policies |
| Moderate | Good trust signals, minor gaps (e.g., no physical address but email available) |
| Weak | Missing key trust signals (no contact info, no policies, no HTTPS) |
| None | Deceptive practices, scam indicators, fake reviews, malware |

## YMYL (Your Money or Your Life)

Topics requiring the highest E-E-A-T standards. Note: E-E-A-T matters everywhere after December 2025, but YMYL topics face the strictest evaluation.

**YMYL categories:**
- Health and safety
- Financial advice and transactions
- Legal information
- News and current events
- Elections and civic trust (added September 2025 QRG)
- Democratic processes (added September 2025 QRG)
- Groups of people (potential for harm)

## Overall E-E-A-T Scoring Guide

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Exceptional — authority site, recognized expert, full transparency |
| 70-89 | Strong — demonstrated expertise, good trust signals |
| 50-69 | Moderate — some signals present, room for improvement |
| 30-49 | Weak — minimal signals, significant gaps |
| 0-29 | Very low — no visible signals, potential trust issues |

### Improvement Recommendations by Score Range

**0-29 (Critical):**
1. Add contact information and about page
2. Establish author identity with credentials
3. Implement HTTPS
4. Remove deceptive elements

**30-49 (Major):**
1. Add author bios with credentials
2. Include first-hand experience content
3. Get external citations/mentions
4. Add customer testimonials

**50-69 (Moderate):**
1. Deepen content with original research
2. Build topical authority through content clusters
3. Pursue industry recognition
4. Document processes and methodologies

**70-89 (Minor):**
1. Maintain freshness with regular updates
2. Expand author presence across platforms
3. Pursue speaking/publication opportunities
4. Add video/multimedia demonstrating expertise

**90-100 (Maintenance):**
1. Continue publishing high-quality content
2. Monitor and respond to reputation issues
3. Keep credentials and certifications current

## Content Minimums by Page Type

These are topical coverage floors, not targets. Google has confirmed word count is NOT a direct ranking factor. The goal is comprehensive topical coverage — a 500-word page that thoroughly answers the query outranks a 2,000-word page that does not.

| Page Type | Min Words | Unique Content % | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Homepage | 500 | 100% | Clearly communicate value proposition |
| Service / Feature Page | 800 | 100% | Detailed explanation of offering |
| Location (Primary) | 600 | 60%+ | City headquarters or main service area |
| Location (Secondary) | 500 | 40%+ | Satellite locations |
| Blog Post | 1,500 | 100% | In-depth, valuable content |
| Product Page | 400 | 80%+ | Unique descriptions, specs (300+ for simple products) |
| Category Page | 400 | 100% | Unique intro, not just product listings |
| About Page | 400 | 100% | Company story, team, values |
| Landing Page | 600 | 100% | Focused conversion content |
| FAQ Page | 800 | 100% | Comprehensive Q&A |

## Location Page Thresholds

### Warning Level (30+ pages)

At 30+ location pages, enforce 60%+ unique content per page. Each page must include:
- Unique local information (landmarks, neighborhoods, local context)
- Location-specific services or offerings
- Local team or staff information
- Genuine customer testimonials from that area

### Hard Stop (50+ pages)

At 50+ location pages, require explicit justification. Must demonstrate:
- Legitimate business presence in each location
- Unique content strategy for each page
- Local signals (Google Business Profile, local reviews)

### Doorway Page Risk

Google's doorway page algorithm penalizes programmatic location pages with thin/duplicate content. Signs of doorway pages:
- Only city/state name changed between pages
- No unique local information
- No local business signals (GBP, local reviews, local phone numbers)
- Keyword-stuffed URLs

## Safe vs Risky Programmatic Pages

### Safe at Scale

| Page Type | Why Safe |
|-----------|----------|
| Integration pages | Real setup documentation, unique technical content |
| Template/tool pages | Downloadable assets, unique functionality |
| Glossary pages | 200+ word unique definitions |
| Product pages | Unique specs, images, reviews |
| User profile pages | User-generated unique content |

### Penalty Risk

| Page Type | Why Risky |
|-----------|-----------|
| Location pages with only city swapped | Duplicate content, doorway pages |
| "Best [tool] for [industry]" | Often thin, no industry-specific value |
| "[Competitor] alternative" | Requires genuine comparison data |
| AI-generated mass content | No unique value, E-E-A-T failure |

## Readability Metrics

### Flesch Reading Ease

Target 60-70 for general audience. Flesch Reading Ease is a useful proxy for content accessibility but is NOT a direct Google ranking factor. John Mueller has confirmed Google does not use basic readability scores for ranking. Yoast deprioritized Flesch scores in v19.3. Use as a content quality indicator, not as an SEO metric to optimize directly.

### Sentence and Paragraph Structure

- Average sentence length: 15-20 words
- Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences
- Grade level: match the target audience
- Use short paragraphs, subheadings, and whitespace for scannability

## Keyword Optimization

### On-Page Placement

- Primary keyword in title tag, H1, and first 100 words
- Natural keyword density: 1-3% (not a hard rule — write naturally)
- Include semantic variations and related terms
- No keyword stuffing — penalized by Google since Panda (2011)

### Content Structure

- Logical heading hierarchy: H1 (one per page) -> H2 -> H3
- Scannable sections with descriptive headings
- Bullet and numbered lists where appropriate
- Table of contents for long-form content (1,500+ words)

---

## Multimedia Requirements

- Include relevant images with proper alt text on every content page
- Alt text: 10-125 characters, describe the image content (not "image.jpg" or keyword stuffing)
- Decorative images: use `alt=""` or `role="presentation"`
- Include videos where appropriate for demonstrating expertise
- Use infographics for complex data visualization
- Include charts/graphs for statistics

---

## Internal Linking Guidelines

| Page Type | Internal Links Target |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Blog post (1,500+ words) | 5-10 internal links |
| Service page | 3-5 internal links |
| Category page | Links to all child pages |
| Product page | 2-4 internal links |

### Anchor Text Rules

- Use descriptive anchor text (never "click here" or "read more")
- Vary anchor text — do not always use exact match keywords
- Link to relevant, related content
- Ensure no orphan pages (every page linked from at least one other page)

### External Linking

- Cite authoritative sources to support claims
- Open external links in new tab for user experience
- Reasonable count — not excessive, not absent

## Title Tag Requirements

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Minimum length | 30 characters |
| Maximum length | 60 characters (Google truncates at approximately 60) |
| Primary keyword | Near the beginning |
| Brand name | At end (if included) |
| Uniqueness | Each page must have a unique title |

### Good Examples
- "Emergency Plumbing Services in Austin | ABC Plumbing"
- "How to Fix a Leaky Faucet: Step-by-Step Guide"

### Bad Examples
- "Home" (too short, not descriptive)
- "Best Plumbing Services for All Your Plumbing Needs in Austin Texas and Surrounding Areas" (too long)
- "ABC Plumbing - Plumbing - Plumber - Plumbing Services" (keyword stuffing)

| Call-to-action | Include a compelling CTA |

## AI Content Assessment (September 2025 QRG)

Google's quality raters now formally evaluate whether content appears AI-generated.

### Acceptable AI Content

- Demonstrates genuine E-E-A-T across all four dimensions
- Provides unique value not available elsewhere
- Has human oversight and editing (not raw AI output)
- Contains original insights, analysis, or perspective
- The presence of AI-generated content is not inherently penalizing

### Low-Quality AI Content Markers

Flag these markers during content analysis:
- Generic phrasing without specificity or concrete details
- No original insight or unique perspective
- No first-hand experience signals
- Factual inaccuracies or hallucinated claims
- Repetitive structure across multiple pages (template-like patterns)
- No author attribution or expertise signals
- Excessive hedging language ("it is important to note that...")

### New Spam Categories (September 2025 QRG)

- **Expired domain abuse:** Buying expired domains for their backlink profile
- **Site reputation abuse:** Using a reputable site to host low-quality third-party content
- **Scaled content abuse:** Mass-producing content without unique value

## Helpful Content System (Merged into Core — March 2024)

The Helpful Content System was merged into Google's core ranking algorithm during the March 2024 core update. It no longer operates as a standalone classifier. Helpfulness signals are now weighted within every core update.

**The same principles apply:**
- Create people-first content that satisfies user intent
- Demonstrate E-E-A-T in every piece of content
- Provide comprehensive answers to the query
- Avoid creating content primarily for search engines

**Enforcement is now continuous** through core updates rather than through separate Helpful Content Updates.

## AI Citation Readiness (GEO Signals)

Optimize content for AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode).

### Core GEO Optimization Strategies

- Write clear, quotable statements with statistics and facts
- Use structured data (especially for data points and factual claims)
- Maintain strong heading hierarchy (H1 -> H2 -> H3 flow)
- Use answer-first formatting for key questions
- Present comparative data in tables and lists
- Include clear attribution and source citations

### Google AI Mode (May 2025)

Google AI Mode launched publicly in May 2025 as a separate tab in Google Search, available in 180+ countries. Unlike AI Overviews (which appear above organic results), AI Mode provides a fully conversational search experience with zero organic blue links — making AI citation the only visibility mechanism.

### Key Optimization Strategies for AI Citation

- **Structured answers:** Clear question-answer formats, definition patterns, step-by-step instructions that AI systems can extract and cite
- **First-party data:** Original research, statistics, case studies, unique datasets are highly cited by AI systems
- **Schema markup:** Article, FAQ (for non-Google AI platforms), structured content schemas help AI systems parse and attribute content
- **Topical authority:** AI systems preferentially cite sources demonstrating deep expertise — build content clusters, not isolated pages
- **Entity clarity:** Ensure brand, authors, and key concepts are clearly defined with structured data (Organization, Person schema)
- **Multi-platform tracking:** Monitor visibility across Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Bing Copilot — treat AI citation as a standalone KPI alongside organic rankings

### Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

GEO is the emerging discipline of optimizing content specifically for AI-generated answers. Key GEO signals:
- **Quotability:** Clear, concise, extractable facts and statements
- **Attribution:** Source citations within content
- **Structure:** Well-organized heading hierarchy
- **Freshness:** Regularly updated data and timestamps

## Content Freshness Signals

| Content Type | Update Frequency |
|--------------|------------------|
| News/current events | Within hours/days |
| Blog posts (evergreen) | Review annually |
| Product pages | When specs change |
| Service pages | Review quarterly |
| Company info | When changes occur |

### Required Elements

- Publication date visible on articles and blog posts
- Last updated date visible if content has been significantly revised
- Changelog for major updates (optional but beneficial for trust)
- Flag content older than 12 months without update for fast-changing topics
