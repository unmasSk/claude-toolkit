# Sales and Revenue Operations Reference

Complete reference for sales enablement collateral, demo scripts, objection handling, proposal templates, sales playbooks, buyer personas, lead lifecycle management, lead scoring, routing, pipeline stages, CRM automation, and RevOps metrics.

---

## Sales Enablement

### Core Principles

**Sales uses what sales trusts.** Involve reps in creation. Use their language, not marketing's. If reps rewrite your deck before sending, you wrote the wrong deck. Test drafts with top performers first.

**Situation-specific, not generic.** Tailor to persona, deal stage, and use case. A deck for a CTO differs from one for a VP of Sales. A post-meeting one-pager serves a different purpose than a trade show handout.

**Scannable over comprehensive.** Reps need information in 3 seconds, not 30. Bold headers, short bullets, visual hierarchy. If a rep cannot find the answer mid-call, the document has failed.

**Tie back to business outcomes.** Every claim connects to revenue, efficiency, or risk reduction. Features mean nothing without the "so what." Replace "AI-powered analytics" with "cut reporting time by 80%."

### Sales Deck / Pitch Deck

#### 10-12 Slide Framework

| Slide | Title | Content |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Current World Problem | The pain your buyer lives with today. A stat quantifying the problem. One core problem only. |
| 2 | Cost of the Problem | Financial impact, time impact, risk impact. What inaction costs over 12 months. Pick 2-3 metrics that hit hardest. |
| 3 | The Shift Happening | Market or technology change creating urgency. Why the old way no longer works. Why now. |
| 4 | Your Approach | Your philosophy and unique point of view. How your approach differs from conventional solutions. |
| 5 | Product Walkthrough | 3-4 key workflows mapped to the pain from slide 1. Screenshots with callouts. Outcomes, not features. |
| 6 | Proof Points | Customer logos (recognizable in their industry). Key metrics: "X% improvement", "Y hours saved". Analyst recognition if relevant. |
| 7 | Case Study | One customer story told well. Challenge, solution, results with before/after metrics. Customer quote. Choose a customer similar to the prospect. |
| 8 | Implementation / Timeline | Clear phases with timeline. What is required from their side vs. yours. Support resources. |
| 9 | ROI / Value | Expected return based on their inputs or benchmarks. Payback period. Total 3-year value. Comparison to cost of inaction. |
| 10 | Pricing Overview | Tiers or structure (3 max). What is included at each level. Recommended plan for their situation. |
| 11 | Next Steps / CTA | Specific next action with timeline. What happens after they say yes. Contact information. |

#### The Storytelling Arc

Every deck follows: **Situation** (slides 1-3) -> **Complication** (slides 2-3) -> **Resolution** (slides 4-11).

The goal is not to present features. The goal is to make the buyer feel understood, then show them a better way.

#### Deck Principles

- Story arc, not feature tour. The deck tells a story: problem, better way, proof, path forward.
- One idea per slide. Two points require two slides.
- Design for presenting, not reading. Slides support conversation; they do not replace it.

#### Customization by Buyer Type

**Technical buyer (CTO, VP Eng):**
- Add: Architecture diagram, security/compliance details, integrations/API, technical implementation requirements.
- Remove: ROI calculations, high-level market trends.
- Tone: Precise, no fluff, respect expertise. Avoid marketing superlatives.

**Economic buyer (CFO, CEO):**
- Add: Detailed ROI with calculations shown, total cost of ownership, risk mitigation, executive summary slide up front.
- Remove: Technical details, feature walkthroughs, implementation specifics.
- Tone: Business-focused, outcome-driven. Speak in dollars and percentages.

**Champion (internal advocate):**
- Add: Internal selling slide (key points for their team), quick-win slide (30-day success), peer proof, objection pre-handling.
- Remove: Deep technical or financial detail, anything requiring context they cannot relay.
- Tone: Empowering, equipping. Make them look smart to their boss.

#### Deck Anti-Patterns

- **Feature dump**: Every slide is a feature with a screenshot. No story, no "so what."
- **Wall of text**: 200+ words per slide. Nobody reads them during presentations.
- **Missing story arc**: Slides in isolation, no narrative flow.
- **Generic screenshots**: Product screenshots without callouts or context.
- **Premature demo**: Features before establishing the problem.
- **Kitchen sink**: 40-slide monster addressing every persona and use case.

### One-Pagers / Leave-Behinds

#### Product Overview One-Pager

```
[Logo]                                              [Tagline]

HEADLINE: One sentence describing what you do and who it is for.

THE PROBLEM
2-3 sentences describing the pain your buyer faces.

THE SOLUTION
2-3 sentences describing how your product solves it.

WHY [YOUR PRODUCT]
- Differentiator 1 -- one sentence benefit
- Differentiator 2 -- one sentence benefit
- Differentiator 3 -- one sentence benefit

PROOF
"Customer quote with specific result." -- Name, Title, Company
[2-3 metric callouts]

[CTA]                    [Contact: name@company.com]
```

#### Use-Case Specific One-Pager

Tailored to a specific workflow, vertical, or problem. Header states the persona ("For Sales Teams"). Structure: headline mapping persona to outcome, the challenge (pain + consequence), how it works (3 numbered steps), results (3 before/after metrics), customer spotlight quote, CTA.

#### Post-Meeting Leave-Behind

Send within 24 hours. Reference specific things they said. Sections: what you discussed (their pain points), how your product helps (mapped solutions), relevant proof (similar customer quote + metrics), proposed next steps (with dates). This is the asset your champion forwards to their boss.

#### Champion Enablement One-Pager

Written in the champion's voice, not yours. Uses "we" and "our" language. Sections: why we are evaluating [product], what it does (plain language), why this solution (3 reasons vs. current state and alternatives), expected impact (current state -> expected state), who else uses it (recognizable names + quote), next steps and what we need from the team.

#### Design Principles

- One page, literally. Front only or front and back maximum.
- Scannable in 30 seconds.
- Bold headers, short bullets, generous whitespace.
- Headline: 18-24pt. Section headers: 12-14pt bold. Body: 10-11pt.
- PDF for sharing, Google Slides/PowerPoint for editable versions.

### Objection Handling

#### Six Objection Categories

| Category | Common Objections |
|----------|------------------|
| Price | "Too expensive." "No budget this quarter." "Competitor is cheaper." |
| Timing | "Not the right time." "Maybe next quarter." "Too busy to implement." |
| Competition | "We already use X." "What makes you different?" |
| Authority | "I need to check with my boss." "The committee decides." |
| Status quo | "What we have works fine." "Not broken, don't fix it." |
| Technical | "Does it integrate with X?" "Security concerns." "Can it scale?" |

#### Response Framework

For every objection, document:
1. **Objection statement**: Exactly how reps hear it.
2. **Why they say it**: The real concern behind the words.
3. **Response approach**: How to acknowledge and redirect.
4. **Proof point**: Specific evidence addressing the concern.
5. **Follow-up question**: Keep the conversation moving forward.

#### Quick-Reference Table (For Live Calls)

| Objection | Response | Proof Point |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| "Too expensive" | "Compared to what? Let's look at what the problem costs you today." | ROI case study showing payback in X months |
| "No budget" | "When budget opens, what needs to be true for this to be a priority?" | Customer who started with a pilot to prove value |
| "Competitor is cheaper" | "They are -- here's what you give up at that price point." | Feature comparison + customer who switched |
| "Not the right time" | "What changes next quarter that makes it better timing?" | Cost-of-delay calculation |
| "Maybe next quarter" | "Happy to reconnect. What would a pilot look like before then?" | Customer who started small and expanded |
| "We use X already" | "How's that working for [specific pain area]?" | Customer who switched from X |
| "What makes you different?" | "For teams like yours, the biggest difference is [specific differentiator]." | Side-by-side comparison |
| "Need to check with my boss" | "What would help you make the case? I can send materials." | Champion one-pager, ROI calculator |
| "The committee decides" | "Who's on the committee and what does each person care about?" | Multi-persona case study |
| "What we have works fine" | "It does -- the question is whether it's costing more than it should." | Benchmark data showing efficiency gaps |
| "Not broken, don't fix it" | "Agreed -- this isn't about fixing, it's about opportunity cost." | Customer who didn't know what they were missing |
| "Does it integrate with X?" | "Yes / Let me check and get specifics by end of day." | Integration docs, customer using same stack |
| "Security concerns" | "Here's our security overview -- happy to loop in our team." | SOC 2 report, security whitepaper |
| "Can it scale?" | "We serve companies from [small] to [large]." | Case study at similar scale |
| "We tried something like this" | "What went wrong? Understanding that helps me show how we're different." | Customer with same failed experience |

#### Detailed Response Patterns

**Price -- "Too expensive":** Do not defend the price. Ask "Compared to what?" Reframe from cost to investment. Walk through ROI together. If budget is real, explore smaller starting points. Follow up: "If the ROI was clear, would this be a priority this quarter?"

**Price -- "Competitor is cheaper":** Acknowledge the price difference. Shift to total cost of ownership and value. Highlight what they lose at the lower price point. Share proof from customers who evaluated both.

**Timing -- "Not the right time":** Understand competing priorities. Quantify cost of waiting (monthly cost of the problem x months delayed). Explore low-commitment next steps. Set concrete follow-up date.

**Competition -- "We already use X":** Do not trash the competitor. Ask how it is working. Probe for specific pain points. Position as complementary or replacement depending on situation. Offer side-by-side comparison.

**Authority -- "Need to check with my boss":** Support, do not pressure. Arm with materials (champion one-pager, ROI calculator). Offer to join a meeting with their boss. Ask what their boss typically prioritizes.

**Authority -- "A committee decides":** Map the buying committee. Provide persona-specific materials. Offer to present to the committee. Help champion navigate internal process. Ask who is most likely to push back.

**Status quo -- "What we have works fine":** Agree. Shift from "broken vs. fixed" to "good vs. great." Introduce opportunity cost. Show what peers are achieving.

**Technical -- "Security concerns":** Take it seriously. Provide documentation proactively (SOC 2, security whitepaper). Offer to connect security teams. Ask about their specific requirements. Offer to fill their security questionnaire.

### ROI Calculators

**Inputs (current state):** Time spent on manual processes, current tool costs, error rates or inefficiency metrics, team size.

**Calculations:** Time saved per week/month/year, cost reduction (tools, headcount, errors), revenue impact (faster deals, higher conversion).

**Outputs:** Annual ROI percentage, payback period in months, total 3-year value.

**Value prop by persona:**

| Persona | Lead With |
|---------|-----------|
| CTO / VP Eng | Technical superiority, integration depth |
| VP Sales | Revenue impact, time savings per rep |
| CFO | ROI, cost reduction, financial predictability |
| End user | Time saved, frustration eliminated |

**Implementation:** Spreadsheet (fastest, easy to customize per deal), web tool (more polished, captures leads), or slide-based (embedded in exec presentations).

### Demo Scripts

#### Discovery Call (30 min)

| Scene | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1. Opening | 3 min | Set agenda, position as peer, get permission to ask questions. "My goal is to understand what you're working on and see if there's a fit -- and if not, I'll tell you that too." |
| 2. Situation Questions | 7 min | Understand current process, tools, team size, how established. "Walk me through how your team handles [process] today." |
| 3. Pain Identification | 10 min | Uncover specific, quantifiable pain. "What's the biggest challenge?" "When that breaks down, what happens?" "How much time per week?" "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?" |
| 4. Impact and Priority | 5 min | Gauge priority, urgency, budget. "Where does solving this sit on your priority list?" "What happens if you don't solve this in 6 months?" |
| 5. Buying Process | 3 min | Map stakeholders, timeline. "What does the evaluation process look like?" "Who else is involved?" |
| 6. Close | 2 min | Summarize 2-3 pain points. Propose specific next step with date. Send calendar invite before hanging up. |

#### First Demo (30-45 min)

| Scene | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1. Opening and Recap | 5 min | Recap discovery findings. Confirm priorities have not changed. Set expectations. |
| 2. Workflow 1 (Primary Pain) | 10 min | Restate pain, show solution step by step, highlight outcome. At 5 min: "How does this compare to how you're handling it today?" |
| 3. Workflow 2 (Secondary Pain) | 8 min | Same structure. "Is this the kind of visibility your team has been asking for?" |
| 4. Workflow 3 (Differentiator) | 7 min | Show something they cannot do today or get from competitors. "How would your team use this?" |
| 5. Proof Point | 3 min | One case study matching their situation. Brief challenge, solution, metrics, quote. |
| 6. Close | 5 min | Recommend specific next step. Identify additional stakeholders. Set follow-up date. Send recap within 2 hours. |

#### Technical Deep-Dive (45-60 min)

| Scene | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1. Opening | 3 min | Confirm top priority for the session. Attendees typically include technical evaluator + solutions engineer. |
| 2. Architecture Overview | 10 min | High-level diagram, infrastructure, data flow, scalability, uptime SLA. |
| 3. Security and Compliance | 10 min | Certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA), encryption, access controls, SSO/RBAC, audit logging, data residency, pen testing. |
| 4. Integrations and API | 15 min | Native integrations, API capabilities (REST, GraphQL, webhooks), auth methods, rate limits, live demo of relevant integration. |
| 5. Implementation and Migration | 5 min | Timeline, phases, data migration, config requirements, training, support model. |
| 6. Q&A and Close | 10 min | Answer directly. Document all questions. Propose next step (security review, POC, pilot). Send technical docs within 24 hours. |

#### Executive Overview (20-30 min)

| Scene | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1. Opening | 2 min | Reference the champion. Set clear agenda. "I'll keep this focused on business impact." |
| 2. Problem and Cost | 5 min | Use their numbers. Connect to revenue, cost, risk. 2-3 key points only. |
| 3. Solution and Differentiation | 5 min | High-level, not feature-level. Tie to strategic priorities. Reference champion's evaluation. |
| 4. ROI and Business Case | 5 min | Walk through ROI calculation. Show the math. Conservative estimates. One strong case study. |
| 5. Q&A and Decision | 5-10 min | Listen more than talk. Get clear next step and timeline. Thank the champion in front of the executive. |

#### Demo Interaction Guidance

**Ask questions after showing each workflow**, when you see a reaction, before moving to the next section, when showing a differentiator, and at the midpoint.

**Do not ask:** "Does that make sense?" (patronizing), "Are you still with me?" (implies lost), "Isn't that cool?" (salesy).

**When prospect asks to see something:** If quick, show it now. If tangential, note it and show after the main flow. If not possible, explain the alternative. Never say "I'll get back to you" without writing it down and following up within 24 hours.

### Case Study Briefs (Sales Format)

Sales case studies differ from marketing case studies. Keep them short, outcome-focused, tagged for retrieval.

**Structure:**
1. Customer profile (industry, company size, buyer role)
2. Challenge (2-3 sentences)
3. Solution (1-2 sentences)
4. Results (3 specific before/after metrics)
5. Pull quote (one sentence)
6. Tags (industry, use case, company size, persona)

Organize by industry, use case, and company size so reps find the right one instantly.

---

### Proposal Templates

**Structure:**
1. Executive summary (their challenge, your solution, expected outcome -- 1 page max)
2. Proposed solution (what you deliver, mapped to their requirements)
3. Implementation plan (timeline, milestones, responsibilities)
4. Investment (pricing, payment terms, what is included)
5. Next steps (how to move forward, decision timeline)

**Rules:** Mirror their language from discovery. Reference specific pain points. Include only relevant case studies. Name the stakeholders. Keep under 10 pages (aim for 5-7). Do not bury the price.

### Sales Playbooks

**Contents:**
- Buyer profile (who you sell to, goals, pains)
- Qualification criteria (BANT, MEDDIC, or your framework)
- Discovery questions organized by topic
- Top 10 objections with responses
- Competitive positioning per competitor
- Demo flow per persona
- Email templates (follow-up, proposal, check-in, breakup)

**Build for:** New product launches (reps need single source of truth), new market segments (different buyers), new hire ramp (cut ramp time). Review quarterly. Assign an owner.

## Revenue Operations (RevOps)

### Core Principles

**Single source of truth.** One CRM as the canonical source. Sync everything to it.

**Define before automate.** Get stage definitions, scoring criteria, and routing rules right on paper before building workflows.

**Measure every handoff.** Marketing-to-sales, SDR-to-AE, AE-to-CS. Each needs an SLA, tracking, and accountability.

**Revenue team alignment.** Marketing, sales, and customer success must agree on definitions. If marketing calls something an MQL but sales will not work it, the definition is wrong.

### Lead Lifecycle (7 Stages)

| Stage | Entry Criteria | Exit Criteria | Owner |
|-------|---------------|---------------|-------|
| **Subscriber** | Opts in to content (blog, newsletter) | Provides company info or shows engagement | Marketing |
| **Lead** | Identified contact with name + email + company | Reaches MQL threshold | Marketing |
| **MQL** | Passes fit + engagement threshold | Sales accepts or rejects within SLA | Marketing -> Sales |
| **SQL** | Sales qualifies via conversation, confirms 2+ of BANT | Opportunity created or recycled | Sales (SDR/AE) |
| **Opportunity** | Formal opportunity in CRM with value, close date, stage | Closed-won or closed-lost | Sales (AE) |
| **Customer** | Closed-won deal, contract signed | Expands, renews, or churns | CS / Account Mgmt |
| **Evangelist** | NPS 9-10, referral activity, case study agreement | Ongoing program participation | CS + Marketing |

**Actions on entry per stage:**

| Stage | Actions on Entry |
|-------|-----------------|
| Subscriber | Add to newsletter nurture. Begin tracking engagement score. |
| Lead | Enrich contact data (company size, industry, role). Begin scoring. Add to relevant nurture sequence. |
| MQL | Instant alert to assigned rep. Create follow-up task with 4-hour SLA. Pause marketing nurture. Log all recent activity for sales context. |
| SQL | Update lifecycle stage in CRM. Notify AE if SDR-qualified. Begin sales sequence if not already in conversation. |
| Opportunity | Add to pipeline reporting. Create deal tasks (proposal, demo, etc.). Notify CS if deal is likely to close. |
| Customer | Trigger onboarding sequence. Assign CS manager. Schedule kickoff call. Remove from all sales sequences. |
| Evangelist | Add to advocacy program. Request case study or testimonial. Invite to referral program. Feature in marketing campaigns (with permission). |

### MQL Definition

An MQL requires both **fit** (who they are) and **engagement** (what they do). Neither alone is sufficient.

**Fit score**: Company size, industry, role, tech stack, geography, revenue.

**Engagement score**: Page visits (especially pricing, demo, case studies), content downloads, webinar attendance, email engagement, product usage (for PLG).

A perfect-fit company that never engages is not an MQL. A student downloading every ebook is not an MQL.

### MQL-to-SQL Handoff SLA

| Metric | Target | Escalation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| First contact attempt | Within 4 business hours | Alert to sales manager at 4 hours |
| Qualification decision | Within 48 hours | Auto-escalate at 48 hours, reassign |
| Meeting scheduled (if qualified) | Within 5 business days | Weekly pipeline review flag |

### SQL-to-Opportunity SLA

| Metric | Target | Escalation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Discovery call completed | Within 3 business days of SQL | Alert to AE manager |
| Opportunity created | Within 5 business days of SQL | Pipeline review flag |

### Opportunity-to-Close SLA

| Metric | Target | Escalation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Proposal delivered | Within 5 business days of demo | AE manager alert |
| Deal stale in stage | 2x average days for that stage | Pipeline review flag |
| Close date pushed 2+ times | Immediate | Forecast review required |

### MQL Criteria by Business Type

**PLG (Product-Led Growth):** Weight 30% fit / 70% engagement. Threshold: 60 points. Recalibrate monthly.
- Key engagement signals: Created free account (+15), completed onboarding (+20), used core feature 3+ times (+25), invited team member (+25), hit usage limit (+20), pricing page visit (+15).

**Enterprise (Sales-Led):** Weight 60% fit / 40% engagement. Threshold: 75 points. Recalibrate quarterly.
- Key fit signals: Company 500+ employees (+20), revenue $50M+ (+15), VP+ title (+20), decision maker confirmed (+15).

**Mid-Market (Hybrid):** Weight 50/50. Threshold: 65 points. Recalibrate quarterly.

#### Negative Scoring

| Signal | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Competitor email domain | -50 |

| Personal email (gmail, yahoo) | -10 |
| Unsubscribe | -20 |
| Hard bounce | -50 |
| Spam complaint | -100 |
| Student/Intern title | -25 |
| No website visit in 90 days | -15 |
| Careers page visitor only | -30 |

#### Threshold Calibration

1. Pull closed-won data from last 6-12 months.
2. Retroactively score each deal with the new model.
3. Find the natural breakpoint separating wins from losses.
4. Set threshold just below where 80% of closed-won deals score.
5. Validate against closed-lost.

**Warning signs model needs recalibration:** MQL-to-SQL acceptance below 30%, sales consistently rejects MQLs, high-scoring leads do not convert while low-scoring leads do, MQL volume spikes without revenue follow-through.

### Lead Routing

#### Routing Methods

| Method | How It Works | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Round-robin | Distribute evenly across reps | Equal territories, similar deal sizes |
| Territory-based | Assign by geography, vertical, or segment | Regional teams, industry specialists |
| Account-based | Named accounts go to named reps | ABM motions, strategic accounts |
| Skill-based | Route by deal complexity, product line, or language | Diverse product lines, global teams |

#### Routing Decision Tree

```
New Lead Arrives
|-- Named/target account? -> Route to assigned account owner
|-- ACV likely > $50K? -> Route to enterprise AE team
|-- PLG signup with team usage? -> Route to PLG sales specialist
|-- Matches a territory? -> Route to territory owner
|-- Default: Round-robin across available reps
    |-- No rep available: Assign to team queue with 1-hour SLA
```

Route to the most specific match first, then fall back to general. Always include a fallback owner. Log every routing decision.

#### Round-Robin Rules

1. Distribute evenly across eligible reps.
2. Skip reps on PTO, at capacity, or with full pipelines.
3. Weight by quota attainment (below-quota reps get slight priority).
4. Reset distribution count weekly or monthly.
5. Log every assignment for auditing.

#### Territory Models

**By geography:** West, Central, East, International teams.

**By company size:** SMB (1-50), Mid-market (51-500), Enterprise (501-5000), Strategic (5000+).

**By industry:** Tech, Financial, Healthcare verticals with specialists; general pool for others.

**Hybrid:** Combine dimensions. Company size > 1000 goes to enterprise team (sub-routed by geography). Industry = Healthcare or Financial goes to vertical specialist. Everything else round-robins across general pool.

#### ABM Routing

| Tier | Account Type | Routing | Response SLA |
|------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| Tier 1 | Top 20 strategic | Named owner, instant alert | 1 hour |
| Tier 2 | Top 100 target | Named owner, standard alert | 4 hours |
| Tier 3 | Target industry/size match | Territory or round-robin | Same business day |

Multi-contact handling: Route all contacts from the same account to the same owner. Trigger "buying committee" alert when 3+ contacts from one account engage.

#### Speed-to-Lead Data

| Response Time | Relative Qualification Rate |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| Under 5 minutes | 21x more likely to qualify |
| 5-10 minutes | 10x |
| 10-30 minutes | 4x |
| 30 min - 1 hour | 2x |
| 1-24 hours | Baseline |
| 24+ hours | 60% lower than baseline |

**Implementation:** Instant notification on MQL creation. Auto-task with 5-minute SLA. Escalation chain: 5 min (original rep), 15 min (backup rep), 30 min (manager), 1 hour (reassign via round-robin). Measure and report weekly.

### Pipeline Stages (7 Stages)

| Stage | Required Fields | Exit Criteria |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Qualified | Contact info, company, source, fit score | Discovery call scheduled |
| Discovery | Pain points, current solution, timeline | Needs confirmed, demo scheduled |
| Demo/Evaluation | Technical requirements, decision makers | Positive evaluation, proposal requested |
| Proposal | Pricing, terms, stakeholder map | Proposal delivered and reviewed |
| Negotiation | Redlines, approval chain, close date | Terms agreed, contract sent |
| Closed Won | Signed contract, payment terms | Handoff to CS complete |
| Closed Lost | Loss reason, competitor (if any) | Post-mortem logged |

#### Stage Hygiene

- **Required fields per stage**: Do not let reps advance without filling required data.
- **Stale deal alerts**: Flag deals sitting in a stage beyond 2x average days.
- **Stage skip detection**: Alert when deals jump stages (Qualified -> Proposal skipping Discovery).
- **Close date discipline**: Push dates require a documented reason.

### CRM Automation

#### Essential Automations

- **Lifecycle stage updates**: Auto-advance when criteria are met.
- **Task creation on handoff**: Follow-up task when MQL assigned to rep (4-hour SLA).
- **SLA alerts**: Notify manager if rep misses response time.
- **Deal stage triggers**: Auto-send proposals, update forecasts, notify CS on close.

#### HubSpot Workflow Recipes

**MQL Alert and Assignment:** Trigger on lifecycle stage change to MQL. Rotate contact owner (round-robin). Send internal email with lead context. Create follow-up task (due in 4 hours). Send Slack notification to #sales-alerts. Enroll in MQL follow-up sequence.

**MQL SLA Escalation:** If MQL not contacted after 12 hours, send warning to owner. After 24 hours, alert manager. After 48 hours, reassign via rotation and create urgent task for new owner.

**Lead Scoring Update and MQL Promotion (Auto-MQL):** Trigger when HubSpot Score reaches threshold. Set lifecycle stage to MQL. Set MQL Date. Suppress from marketing nurture. Trigger MQL Alert workflow. Add suppression for existing customers and competitors.

**Meeting Booked:** On Calendly/HubSpot meeting, notify contact owner, update lifecycle stage to MQL if still Lead, create prep task (1 hour before meeting), include recent page views in notification. Send Slack to #meetings channel.

**Closed-Won Handoff:** Update lifecycle to Customer. Set Customer Since date. Assign CS team member. Create kickoff task (due 2 business days). Enroll in onboarding sequence. Send internal notification to CS manager. Remove from all sales sequences.

**Stale Deal Alert:** When deal exceeds 2x average days in stage, alert owner. Create update task (3 days). After 7 days with no update, alert manager. Customize thresholds per stage (Discovery: 14 days, Proposal: 10 days, Negotiation: 21 days).

**Lead Activity Digest:** Scheduled daily at 8 AM. Filter contacts: SQL or Opportunity stage with website activity in last 24 hours. Send digest to each rep with pages visited, content downloaded, emails opened/clicked. Exclude single homepage visits.

**Recycled Lead Nurture:** On sales rejection, update to Recycled stage. Reset engagement score (keep fit score). Enroll in lower-frequency nurture. Set re-enrollment trigger: if score re-crosses threshold, re-trigger MQL workflow. Track recycled-to-MQL conversion rate separately.

#### Salesforce Flow Equivalents

**MQL Alert and Assignment:** Record-Triggered Flow on Lead Status change to MQL. Query Rep Assignment custom object for next available rep. Assign Lead Owner. Create Task due NOW + 4 hours. Send email alert to new owner. Update rep assignment timestamp.

**SLA Escalation:** Scheduled-Triggered Flow every 4 hours during business hours. Get Leads where Status = MQL and LastActivityDate < TODAY - 1. Decision: older than 48 hours with no activity? Yes → reassign, create urgent task, alert manager. No → send reminder to current owner.

**Pipeline Stage Automation:** Record-Triggered Flow on Opportunity Stage change. Per stage: Discovery → create questionnaire task; Demo → create prep task; Proposal → create send task + deal desk alert if ACV > $25K; Closed Won → trigger CS handoff; Closed Lost → create loss reason task.

**Stale Deal Detection:** Scheduled-Triggered Flow daily at 7 AM. Get open Opportunities where Days_In_Stage > Stage_SLA_Threshold. Create task and email owner. If > 2x threshold, email manager. Set Stale Flag = true for dashboard visibility.

#### Zapier Cross-Tool Patterns

**New Lead → CRM + Slack + Task:** Trigger on new form submission (Typeform, HubSpot, Webflow). Create/update CRM contact. Enrich with Clearbit. Post to #new-leads Slack with enriched data. Create task in project management tool.

**Meeting Booked → CRM + Prep Email:** Trigger on new Calendly/SavvyCal booking. Find or create CRM contact. Update lifecycle to MQL. Send prep email to rep with CRM link, LinkedIn profile, recent activity.

**Deal Closed → Onboarding Stack:** Trigger on CRM stage = Closed Won. Create customer record in CS tool (Vitally, Gainsight). Add to onboarding project. Send welcome email. Create Slack channel #customer-[company]. Notify CS team.

**Lead Scoring → Cross-Tool Sync:** Trigger on CRM score crossing MQL threshold. Update marketing automation status. Add to retargeting audience (Facebook, Google Ads). Trigger SDR outreach sequence. Log event in analytics platform.

**SLA Breach → Multi-Channel Alert:** Trigger on overdue MQL follow-up task. Slack DM to rep. Email to rep. If 2+ hours overdue → Slack DM to manager. If 4+ hours overdue → reassign in CRM via webhook.

**Weekly Pipeline Digest:** Scheduled every Monday at 8 AM. Query CRM for pipeline summary (total value, new deals, stale deals, expected closes). Post to #sales-team Slack. Send email digest to sales leadership.

#### Scheduling Tool Integration (Calendly/SavvyCal)

**Round-robin setup:** Create team event type. Distribution: optimize for equal distribution. Buffer: 15 min before/after. Minimum notice: 4 hours.

**CRM integration:** Webhook on booking -> match email to CRM contact -> if existing, assign to contact owner (override round-robin) -> if new, create lead via routing rules -> set lifecycle to MQL (meeting = high intent).

**No-show workflow:** 30 min after scheduled time, check if meeting was logged. If not, send "Sorry we missed you" email. Create reschedule task. On second no-show, flag and alert manager.

### Data Hygiene

**Dedup strategy:** Match on email domain + company name + phone. CRM record wins over marketing automation. Most recent activity wins for fields. Run weekly automated dedup with manual review.

**Required fields enforcement:** Enforce at each lifecycle stage. Block advancement if fields are empty. Use progressive profiling.

**Quarterly audit:**
- Review and merge duplicates
- Validate email deliverability on stale contacts
- Archive contacts with no activity in 12+ months
- Audit lifecycle stage distribution (look for bottlenecks)
- Verify enrichment data accuracy on a sample set

### Lead Rejection and Recycling

**Rejection reason codes:**

| Code | Reason | Recycle Action |
|------|--------|----------------|
| FIT-01 | Company too small | Nurture; re-score if company grows |
| FIT-02 | Wrong industry | Archive; do not recycle |
| FIT-03 | Wrong role / no authority | Nurture; monitor for org changes |
| ENG-01 | No response after 3 attempts | Recycle to nurture in 90 days |
| ENG-02 | Interested but bad timing | Recycle in 60 days |
| QUAL-01 | No budget | Recycle in 90 days |
| QUAL-02 | Using competitor, locked in | Trigger before contract renewal |
| QUAL-03 | Not a real project | Archive; do not recycle |

**Recycling workflow:** Sales rejects with reason code -> CRM updates to Recycled -> lower-frequency nurture (bi-weekly or monthly) -> engagement score resets (keep fit score) -> if re-crosses MQL threshold, re-route with Recycled MQL flag -> track recycled conversion rate separately.

### RevOps Metrics Dashboard

| Metric | Formula / Definition | Benchmark |
|--------|---------------------|-----------|
| Lead-to-MQL rate | MQLs / Total leads | 5-15% |
| MQL-to-SQL rate | SQLs / MQLs | 30-50% |

| Pipeline coverage ratio | Pipeline value / quota | 3-4x target |
| Stage conversion rates | Show where deals die | Per stage |
| Average time in stage | Show where deals stall | Per stage |

**Build three dashboard views:**
1. **Marketing view**: Lead volume, MQL rate, source attribution, cost per MQL.

## Composio MCP for CRM Platforms

For CRM platforms without native MCP servers, Composio provides MCP access.

**Setup:** `npx @composio/mcp@latest setup`. Authenticate via Connect Link on first use.

### HubSpot via Composio

**Toolkit:** `HUBSPOT` | **Auth:** OAuth 2.0

Capabilities: Read/write contacts, deals, companies, lists. Trigger workflows. Manage email sequences. Run reports. Create tasks.

Use for: Lead lifecycle management, MQL routing automation, pipeline reporting, contact enrichment sync.

### Salesforce via Composio

**Toolkit:** `SALESFORCE` | **Auth:** OAuth 2.0

Capabilities: Execute SOQL queries, create/update objects, manage leads and opportunities, run reports, trigger flows.

Use for: Enterprise pipeline management, lead scoring sync, deal desk automation, cross-object reporting.

For enrichment operations (Apollo, Clearbit, ZoomInfo), use the CLI tools directly. They provide more granular control over search parameters and bulk operations. Composio is most valuable for CRM read/write operations that would otherwise require managing OAuth tokens manually.
