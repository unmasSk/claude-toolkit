# Growth Reference

Complete reference for launch strategy, lead magnets, free tool strategy, and referral programs. Covers the full growth playbook from product launch through viral loops.

---

## Launch Strategy

### The ORB Framework

Structure launch marketing across three channel types. Drive all traffic back to owned channels.

#### Owned Channels

Channels where you have direct access without algorithms or platform rules. They get more effective over time and compound in value.

**Types:**
- Email list
- Blog
- Podcast
- Branded community (Slack, Discord)
- Website/product

**Selection guidance:**
- Industry lacks quality content: start a blog.
- People want direct updates: focus on email.
- Engagement matters: build a community.
- Start with 1-2 channels based on where your audience already pays attention.

**Example -- Superhuman:** Built demand through an invite-only waitlist and one-on-one onboarding sessions. Every new user received a 30-minute live demo. This created exclusivity, FOMO, and word-of-mouth through owned relationships.

#### Rented Channels

Platforms that provide visibility but you do not control. Algorithms shift, rules change, pay-to-play increases. Use them to drive traffic to owned channels.

**Types:**
- Social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram)
- App stores and marketplaces
- YouTube
- Reddit

**How to use correctly:**
- Pick 1-2 platforms where your audience is active.
- Use them to drive traffic to owned channels, not as your primary strategy.
- Every viral post must lead to a signup, email capture, or owned channel entry.

**Platform-specific tactics:**
- Twitter/X: Threads that spark conversation, link to newsletter.
- LinkedIn: High-value posts, lead to gated content or email signup.
- Marketplaces (Shopify, Slack): Optimize listing, drive to site for deeper engagement.

**Example -- Notion:** Leveraged Twitter, YouTube, and Reddit where productivity enthusiasts were active. Encouraged community template sharing. Funneled all visibility into signups and targeted email onboarding.

#### Borrowed Channels

Tap into someone else's audience to shortcut audience building.

**Types:**
- Guest content (blog posts, podcast interviews, newsletter features)
- Collaborations (webinars, co-marketing, social takeovers)
- Speaking engagements (conferences, panels, virtual summits)
- Influencer partnerships

**Execution approach:**
1. List industry leaders your audience follows.
2. Pitch win-win collaborations.
3. Use tools like SparkToro or Listen Notes to find audience overlap.
4. Set up affiliate/referral incentives for ongoing promotion.

**Example -- TRMNL:** Sent a free e-ink display to YouTuber Snazzy Labs (not a paid sponsorship). He created an in-depth review: 500K+ views, $500K+ in sales. They also set up an affiliate program for ongoing promotion.

Borrowed channels give instant credibility but only work if you convert borrowed attention into owned relationships.

### Five-Phase Launch Approach

#### Phase 1: Internal Launch

Gather initial feedback and iron out major issues before going public.

- Recruit early users one-on-one to test for free.
- Collect feedback on usability gaps and missing features.
- Ensure prototype is functional enough to demo (does not need to be production-ready).
- **Goal:** Validate core functionality with friendly users.

#### Phase 2: Alpha Launch

Put the product in front of external users in a controlled way.

- Create landing page with early access signup form.
- Announce the product exists.
- Invite users individually to start testing.
- MVP should be working in production (even if still evolving).
- **Goal:** First external validation and initial waitlist building.

#### Phase 3: Beta Launch

Scale up early access while generating external buzz.

- Work through early access list (some free, some paid).
- Start marketing with teasers about problems you solve.
- Recruit friends, investors, and influencers to test and share.
- Consider adding: coming soon page, "Beta" badge in UI, email invites, early access toggle for experimental features.
- **Goal:** Build buzz and refine product with broader feedback.

#### Phase 4: Early Access Launch

Shift from small-scale testing to controlled expansion.

- Leak product details: screenshots, feature GIFs, demos.
- Gather quantitative usage data and qualitative feedback.
- Run user research with engaged users (incentivize with credits).
- Optionally run product/market fit survey to refine messaging.
- Expansion options: throttle invites in batches (5-10% at a time), or invite all users at once under "early access" framing.
- **Goal:** Validate at scale and prepare for full launch.

#### Phase 5: Full Launch

Open the floodgates.

- Open self-serve signups.
- Start charging (if not already).
- Announce general availability across all channels.

**Launch touchpoints:**
- Customer emails
- In-app popups and product tours
- Website banner linking to launch assets
- "New" badge in dashboard navigation
- Blog post announcement
- Social posts across platforms
- Product Hunt, BetaList, Hacker News, etc.

- **Goal:** Maximum visibility and conversion to paying users.

### Product Hunt Launch Strategy

**Pros:** Exposure to tech-savvy early adopters, credibility bump (especially Product of the Day), potential PR coverage and backlinks.

**Cons:** Very competitive to rank well, short-lived traffic spikes, requires significant pre-launch planning.

**Before launch day:**
1. Build relationships with influential supporters, content hubs, and communities.
2. Optimize listing: compelling tagline, polished visuals, short demo video.
3. Study successful launches to identify what worked.
4. Engage in relevant communities and provide value before pitching.
5. Prepare your team for all-day engagement.

**On launch day:**
1. Treat it as an all-day event.
2. Respond to every comment in real-time.
3. Answer questions and spark discussions.
4. Encourage your existing audience to engage.
5. Direct traffic back to your site to capture signups.

**After launch day:**
1. Follow up with everyone who engaged.
2. Convert Product Hunt traffic into owned relationships (email signups).
3. Continue momentum with post-launch content.

**Case study -- SavvyCal:** Optimized landing page and onboarding before launch. Built relationships with productivity/SaaS influencers in advance. Responded to every comment. Result: #2 Product of the Month.

**Case study -- Reform:** Studied successful launches and applied insights. Clear tagline, polished visuals, demo video. Engaged communities before launch. Treated launch as all-day engagement event. Result: #1 Product of the Day.

### Post-Launch Product Marketing

**Immediate actions:**
- Set up automated onboarding email sequence introducing key features and use cases.
- Include announcement in weekly/biweekly/monthly roundup email.
- Publish comparison pages highlighting differentiation.
- Add dedicated sections about the new feature across your site.
- Create no-code interactive demo (using tools like Navattic) for pre-signup exploration.

**Ongoing Launch Strategy**

Do not rely on a single launch event. Regular updates and feature rollouts sustain engagement.

**Update prioritization matrix:**
- **Major updates** (new features, product overhauls): Full campaign across multiple channels. Blog post, email campaign, in-app messages, social media.
- **Medium updates** (new integrations, UI enhancements): Targeted announcement. Email to relevant segments, in-app banner.
- **Minor updates** (bug fixes, small tweaks): Changelog and release notes. Signal active development.

**Tactics:** Space out releases. Reuse high-performing tactics. Continue using email, social, and in-app messaging. Even small changelog updates remind customers your product is evolving, building retention and word-of-mouth.

## Lead Magnets

### Lead Magnet Principles

1. **Solve a specific problem.** Address one clear pain point. "How to write cold emails that get replies" beats "Marketing guide."
2. **Match the buyer stage.** Awareness leads need education. Consideration leads need comparison. Decision leads need implementation help.
3. **High perceived value, low time investment.** Should look worth paying for. Consumable in under 30 minutes (ideally under 10). Immediate, actionable takeaway.
4. **Natural path to product.** Solves a problem your product also solves, creates awareness of a gap your product fills, or demonstrates expertise.
5. **Easy to consume.** One clear format. Works on mobile. No special software required.

### Lead Magnet Types

| Type | Best For | Effort | Time to Create |
|------|----------|--------|----------------|
| Checklist | Quick wins, process steps | Low | 1-2 hours |
| Cheat sheet | Reference material, shortcuts | Low | 2-4 hours |
| Template (doc/spreadsheet/Notion) | Repeatable processes, workflows | Low-Med | 2-8 hours |
| Swipe file | Inspiration, curated examples | Medium | 4-8 hours |
| Ebook/guide | Deep education, authority building | High | 1-3 weeks |
| Mini-course (email) | Education + nurture | Medium | 1-2 weeks |
| Mini-course (video) | Education + personality | High | 2-4 weeks |
| Quiz/assessment | Segmentation, engagement | Medium | 1-2 weeks |
| Webinar | Authority, live engagement | Medium | 1 week prep |
| Resource library | Ongoing value, return visits | High | Ongoing |

### Format Creation Guidelines

**Checklists:** 1-2 pages. Numbered or checkbox items. Actionable language ("Verify X", "Set up Y"). Group into sections if 10+ items. Make printable.

**Cheat sheets:** One page (two max). Organized by category. Dense but scannable. Tables, grids, or columns. Optimized for quick reference.

**Templates:** Include "How to Use" instructions. Pre-fill with example data. Make usable within 5 minutes. For spreadsheets: use data validation, conditional formatting, lock formula cells. For Notion: provide duplicate link with getting-started guide.

**Swipe files:** 15-50 curated examples. Each with the example itself, why it works (2-3 bullets), and how to adapt it. Quality over quantity.

**Ebooks/guides:** 10-25 pages. 3-7 chapters, one concept per chapter. Include visuals. End each chapter with a takeaway. CTA at the end. Density beats length.

**Mini-courses (email):** 3-5 emails over 5-7 days. One lesson per email. Teach, example, exercise pattern. Progressive difficulty. Final email: summary + product CTA.

**Mini-courses (video):** 3-5 videos, 5-15 minutes each. Host on unlisted YouTube, Loom, or a course platform. Deliver links via email drip. Include worksheets or exercises per lesson. More personal — builds stronger connection. Cadence: every 1-2 days.

**Quizzes/assessments:** 5-10 questions (sweet spot: 7). Multiple choice only. 3-5 result categories with personalized recommendations. Gate results behind email capture (quiz itself is ungated).

**Live webinars:** 30-45 minutes teaching + 15 minutes Q&A. Structure: Hook, Teach (3 key points), Demo/example, CTA. Send 3 reminders (confirmation, day before, 1 hour before). Record for replay.

**Evergreen webinars:** Pre-recorded, available on demand. Same structure as live but tighter editing. Always-on lead generation. Gate with email registration. Automated follow-up sequence.

### Matching Lead Magnets to Buyer Stage

**Awareness:** Checklists, cheat sheets, ebooks, quizzes. Example: "10-Point Website Audit Checklist."

**Consideration:** Comparison templates, assessments, case study collections, webinars. Example: "CRM Comparison Spreadsheet."

**Decision:** Templates, free trials, implementation guides, ROI calculators. Example: "Migration Checklist: Switch in 30 Minutes."

### Gating Strategy

| Approach | When to Use | Trade-off |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Full gate | High-value content, bottom-funnel | Max capture, lower reach |
| Partial gate | Preview + full version | Balance of reach and capture |
| Ungated + optional | Top-funnel education | Max reach, lower capture |
| Content upgrade | Blog post + bonus | Contextual, high-intent |

**What to ask for:**
- **Email only**: Highest conversion, lowest friction.
- **Email + name**: Enables personalization, slight friction increase.
- **Email + company/role**: Better qualification, more friction.
- **Multi-field**: Only for high-value offers (webinars, demos).

Rule: Every extra field reduces conversion by 5-10%.

**Framing the exchange:** Make value obvious ("Get the full 25-page guide free"). Show a preview. Add social proof ("Downloaded by 5,000+ marketers"). Reduce risk ("No spam. Unsubscribe anytime.").

### Landing Page Structure

1. **Headline**: Clear benefit -- what they get and why it matters.
2. **Preview/mockup**: Visual of the lead magnet.
3. **What's inside**: 3-5 bullet points of key takeaways.
4. **Social proof**: Download count, testimonials, logos.
5. **Form**: Minimal fields, clear CTA button.
6. **FAQ**: Address hesitations.

### Delivery Methods

| Method | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Instant download | Immediate gratification | No email verification |
| Email delivery | Verifies email, starts relationship | Slight delay |
| Thank you page + email | Instant access + email copy | Slightly more complex |
| Drip delivery | Builds habit, multiple touchpoints | Only for courses/series |

**Thank you page optimization:** Confirm delivery. Offer a next step (demo, trial, community). Include social share buttons (pre-written post). Recommend related content.

### Promotion and Distribution

- **Blog CTAs and content upgrades**: Add relevant CTAs within posts. Create post-specific upgrades. Content upgrades convert 2-5x better than generic sidebar CTAs.
- **Exit-intent popups**: Trigger on exit intent or scroll depth. Match the popup offer to the page content.
- **Social media**: Share snippets and teasers. Create carousel posts from key points. Use lead magnet as bio CTA.
- **Paid promotion**: Facebook/Instagram lead ads for top-funnel. Google Ads for high-intent (templates, tools). LinkedIn for B2B. Retarget blog visitors.
- **Partner co-promotion**: Cross-promote with complementary brands. Guest webinars. Include in partner newsletters.

## Free Tool Strategy

### Core Principles

1. **Solve a real problem.** The tool must provide genuine value, useful even without your main product.
2. **Adjacent to core product.** Related to what you sell. Natural path from tool to product.
3. **Simple and focused.** Does one thing well. Low friction. Immediate value.
4. **Worth the investment.** Lead value x expected leads must exceed build cost + maintenance.

### Tool Types

| Type | Examples | Best For |
|------|----------|----------|
| Calculators | ROI, savings, pricing, cost comparison, break-even | Decisions involving numbers. Personalized output, high perceived value, share-worthy. |
| Generators | Templates, policies, names, email subjects, contracts | Creating something quickly. Tangible output, saves time, repeat usage. |
| Analyzers/Auditors | Website graders, SEO analyzers, headline analyzers, security checkers | Evaluating existing work. Curiosity-driven, creates awareness of problems, natural lead to solution. |
| Testers/Validators | Meta tag preview, email rendering, speed test, DNS checker, broken link finder | Checking if something works. Bookmark-worthy, repeat usage, professional necessity. |
| Libraries/Resources | Icon sets, template libraries, code snippets, example galleries | Reference material. High SEO value, ongoing traffic, linkable asset. |
| Interactive Educational | Tutorials, playgrounds, quizzes, simulators, decision trees, configurators | Learning/understanding. Deep engagement, demonstrates expertise, shareable. |

### Tool Concepts by Industry

**SaaS:** Product ROI calculator, competitor comparison tool, readiness assessment quiz, template library, feature configurator.

**Agency/Services:** Industry benchmark tool, project scoping calculator, cost estimator, proposal generator.

**E-commerce:** Product finder quiz, comparison tool, size/fit calculator, savings calculator.

**Developer Tools:** Code snippet library, testing/preview tool, documentation generator, API playground.

**Finance:** Financial calculators, investment comparison tool, budget planner, tax estimator, loan calculator.

### Ideation Framework

1. **What problems does your audience Google?** Research search queries and common questions.
2. **What manual processes are tedious?** Spreadsheet tasks, repetitive calculations.
3. **What do they need before buying your product?** Assessments, planning, comparisons.
4. **What information do they wish they had?** Data they cannot easily access, benchmarks.

### Validation

- **Search demand**: Is there volume? How competitive?
- **Uniqueness**: What exists? How can you be 10x better?
- **Lead quality**: Does this audience match buyers?
- **Build feasibility**: How complex? Can you scope an MVP?

### Lead Capture Strategy

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| Fully gated | Maximum capture | Lower usage |
| Partially gated (preview free, full results gated) | Balance of both | Most common pattern |
| Ungated + optional email | Maximum reach | Lower capture |
| Ungated entirely | Pure SEO/brand play | No direct leads |

Best practices: Clear value exchange ("Get your full report"). Minimal friction (email only). Show preview of gated results. Optionally segment by adding one qualifying question.

### SEO Strategy for Tools

**Landing page keywords:** "[thing] calculator", "[thing] generator", "free [tool type]"

**Supporting content:** "How to [use case]", "What is [concept]"

Free tools attract backlinks because they are genuinely useful, unique, and shareable.

### Build vs. Buy

- **Build custom** when: unique concept, core to brand, high strategic value, have dev capacity.
- **Use no-code tools** when: speed to market, limited dev resources, testing concept. Options: Outgrow, Involve.me, Typeform, Tally, Bubble, Webflow.
- **Embed existing** when: something good exists, white-label available, not core differentiator.

### MVP Scope

Include: core functionality, essential UX (clear input, obvious output, mobile-ready), basic lead capture.

Skip initially: account creation, saving results, advanced features, perfect design, edge cases.

### Evaluation Scorecard

Rate 1-5 per factor:

| Factor | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Search demand exists | ___ |
| Audience match to buyers | ___ |
| Uniqueness vs. existing tools | ___ |
| Natural path to product | ___ |
| Build feasibility | ___ |
| Maintenance burden (inverse) | ___ |
| Link-building potential | ___ |
| Share-worthiness | ___ |

**25+**: Strong candidate. **15-24**: Promising. **Under 15**: Reconsider.

## Referral Programs

### Referral vs. Affiliate Programs

**Customer Referral Programs:**
- Referrer is an existing customer.
- Best for: products with natural word-of-mouth, lower-ticket or self-serve products.
- One-time or limited rewards. Higher trust, lower volume.

**Affiliate Programs:**
- Affiliates may not be customers. Content creators, influencers, bloggers.
- Best for: reaching audiences you lack access to, higher-ticket products that justify commissions.
- Ongoing commission relationship. Higher volume, variable trust.

### The Referral Loop

```
Trigger Moment -> Share Action -> Convert Referred User -> Reward -> (Loop)
```

### Step 1: Identify Trigger Moments

Prompt referral at high-intent moments:
- Right after first "aha" moment
- After achieving a milestone
- After exceptional support experience
- After renewing or upgrading

### Step 2: Design Share Mechanism

Ranked by conversion effectiveness:
1. In-product sharing (highest conversion)
2. Personalized referral link
3. Email invitation
4. Social sharing
5. Referral code (works offline)

### Step 3: Choose Incentive Structure

**Single-sided rewards (referrer only):** Simpler to implement. Works for high-value products where the referral itself is compelling.

**Double-sided rewards (both parties):** Higher conversion rate. Win-win framing removes friction for the referred user.

**Tiered rewards:** Gamifies the referral process. Increases engagement over time. Example -- Morning Brew: 3 referrals = stickers, 5 = t-shirt, 10 = mug, 25 = hoodie.

### Incentive Types Comparison

| Type | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|------|------|------|----------|
| Cash/credit | Universally valued | Feels transactional | Marketplaces, fintech |
| Product credit | Drives usage | Only valuable if they use it | SaaS, subscriptions |
| Free months | Clear value | May attract freebie-seekers | Subscription products |
| Feature unlock | Low cost to you | Only for gated features | Freemium products |
| Swag/gifts | Memorable, shareable | Logistics complexity | Brand-focused companies |
| Charity donation | Feel-good | Lower personal motivation | Mission-driven brands |

### Incentive Sizing Framework

```
Max Referral Reward = (Customer LTV x Gross Margin) - Target CAC
```

Example: LTV $1,200, Gross margin 70%, Target CAC $200. Max reward = ($1,200 x 0.70) - $200 = $640.

**Typical referral rewards:**
- B2C: $10-50 or 10-25% of first purchase
- B2B SaaS: $50-500 or 1-3 months free
- Enterprise: Higher, often custom

### Program Examples

**Dropbox:** Give 500MB, get 500MB. Reward tied to product value. Low friction. Both parties benefit equally. Gamified with progress tracking.

**Uber/Lyft:** Give $10 ride credit, get $10 when they ride. Immediate value. Double-sided. Easy to share. Triggered at natural moments.

**Notion:** $10 credit per referral (education). Targeted high-sharing audience (students). Product naturally spreads in teams.

### Optimization

**If few customers are referring:**
- Ask at better moments
- Simplify sharing to one click
- Test different incentive types
- Make referral prominent in product

**If referrals are not converting:**
- Improve landing experience for referred users
- Strengthen incentive for new users
- Ensure referrer's endorsement is visible on landing page

**A/B tests to run:** Incentive amount, type, single vs. double-sided, timing. Program description and CTA copy. Placement and timing of referral prompt.

### Measuring Success

**Program health metrics:**
- Active referrers (referred someone in last 30 days)
- Referral conversion rate
- Rewards earned/paid

**Business impact metrics:**
- % of new customers from referrals
- CAC via referral vs. other channels
- LTV of referred customers (typically 16-25% higher)
- Referral program ROI

**Viral coefficient (K-factor):**
```
K = Invitations sent per user x Conversion rate of invitations
K > 1 = Viral growth (each user brings more than 1 new user)
K < 1 = Amplified growth (referrals supplement other acquisition)
```

**Benchmarks:**
- Good referral rate: 10-25% of customers refer
- Great: 25-50%
- Average referrals per referrer: 1-2. Good: 2-5.
- Referred customers have 18-37% lower churn
- Referred customers refer others at 2-3x rate

**ROI calculation:**
```
Referral Program ROI = (Revenue from referred customers - Program costs) / Program costs
Program costs = Rewards paid + Tool costs + Management time
```

### Affiliate Program Design

**Commission structures:**
- Percentage of sale: 10-30% of first sale or first year.
- Flat fee per action: $5-500 depending on value.
- Recurring commission: 10-25% of recurring revenue for 12+ months.
- Tiered commission: 20% for 1-10 sales, 25% for 11-25, 30% for 26+.

**Cookie duration:**

| Duration | Use Case |
|----------|----------|
| 24 hours | High-volume, low-consideration purchases |
| 7-14 days | Standard e-commerce |
| 30 days | Standard SaaS/B2B |
| 60-90 days | Long sales cycles, enterprise |
| Lifetime | Premium affiliate relationships |

**Affiliate recruitment sources:** Existing customers who create content, industry bloggers/reviewers, YouTubers, newsletter writers, complementary tool companies, consultants and agencies.

**Affiliate outreach template:**

```
Subject: Partnership opportunity — [Your Product]

Hi [Name],

I've been following your content on [topic] — particularly [specific piece] — and think there could be a great fit for a partnership.

[Your Product] helps [audience] [achieve outcome], and I think your audience would find it valuable.

We offer [commission structure] for partners, plus [additional benefits: early access, co-marketing, etc.].

Would you be open to learning more?

[Your name]
```

**Affiliate enablement (provide):** Unique tracking links/codes, product overview and key benefits, target audience description, competitor comparison, creative assets, sample copy, case studies, demo access, FAQ and objection handling, payment terms.

**Affiliate program tools:**

| Category | Platforms |
|----------|-----------|
| Affiliate networks | ShareASale, Impact (enterprise), PartnerStack (SaaS), Tapfiliate, FirstPromoter |
| Self-hosted | Rewardful (Stripe-integrated), Refersion (e-commerce) |
| Referral platforms | ReferralCandy (e-commerce), GrowSurf (SaaS), Mention Me, Viral Loops, Friendbuy |

### Fraud Prevention

**Common fraud:** Self-referrals (fake accounts), referral rings, coupon sites posting codes, fake emails, VPN/device spoofing.

**Prevention measures:**
- Email verification required
- Device fingerprinting and IP monitoring
- Delayed reward payout (after activation or meaningful action)
- Minimum activity threshold
- Clear terms of service
- Maximum referrals per period
- Reward clawback for refunds/chargebacks

### Email Sequences

**Referral program launch email:**
```
Subject: You can now earn [reward] for sharing [Product]

We just launched our referral program!
Share [Product] with friends and earn [reward] for each signup.
They get [their reward] too.

[Unique referral link]

1. Share your link
2. Friend signs up
3. You both get [reward]
```

**Referral nurture sequence:**
- Day 7: Remind about referral program
- Day 30: "Know anyone who'd benefit?"
- Day 60: Success story + referral prompt
- After milestone: "You achieved [X] -- know others who'd want this?"

## Composio MCP for Growth Tools

For tools lacking native MCP servers, Composio provides MCP access via a single server.

**Setup:** `npx @composio/mcp@latest setup`. Authenticate each tool via Connect Link on first use.

**Relevant Composio integrations:**

| Tool | Composio Toolkit | Use Case |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| HubSpot | `HUBSPOT` | Landing page forms, lead capture, email sequences for lead magnets |
| Google Sheets | `GOOGLESHEETS` | Referral tracking spreadsheets, launch checklists |
| Slack | `SLACK` | Launch day coordination, referral alerts |
| Notion | `NOTION` | Template distribution, launch planning |

For referral and affiliate platform operations, use the CLI tools directly (rewardful.js, tolt.js, mention-me.js, partnerstack.js, dub.js). Composio is most valuable for CRM and cross-tool automation surrounding growth campaigns.
